# 01 - Product Overview

[← Back to PRD](../PRD.md)

---

## Vision

**Peace of mind for everyone living alone.**

A world where no one dies unnoticed. Where family and friends can rest easy knowing they'll be alerted if something goes wrong.

---

## Mission

Provide the simplest possible safety net for solo dwellers through automated check-ins and emergency alerts.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary Segments

| Segment | Demographics | Motivation |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| **Young Professionals** | Ages 25-40, urban, living alone | Parents worry about them; want to provide reassurance |
| **Students** | Ages 18-25, living away from home | First time living independently; safety net for parents |
| **Elderly** | Ages 65+, living independently | Children live far away; health concerns |
| **Health-Conscious** | Any age, with medical conditions | Higher risk of sudden emergencies |
| **Mental Health** | Introverts, those with depression | May isolate; extra vulnerability |

### Geographic Focus
- **Launch Market**: United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia
- **Language**: English only (v1.0)
- **Expansion**: Spanish, other European languages (v2.0+)

---

## Market Opportunity

### Solo Living Trends

| Region | Statistic |
|--------|-----------|
| China | 200M single-person households projected by 2030 |
| United States | 37M people live alone (28% of households) |
| United Kingdom | 8.2M single-person households |
| Germany | 42% of households are single-person |

### Proof of Concept
The original Chinese app "死了么" (Are You Dead?) achieved:
- #1 paid app in China (January 2026)
- International downloads in US, Singapore, Hong Kong, Australia, Spain
- Valuation: 10 million yuan (~$1.4M USD)
- Development cost: ~$150 USD

**This validates strong market demand for this exact solution.**

---

## Competitive Analysis

| Competitor | Type | Difference |
|------------|------|------------|
| **死了么 (Demumu)** | Direct inspiration | Chinese-focused; we target English markets |
| **Life360** | Family location tracking | Feature-heavy; location-focused; subscription model |
| **Apple Check In** | iOS feature | Requires iMessage; manual activation each time |
| **Manual wellness calls** | Human behavior | Unreliable; social friction; easy to forget |
| **Medical alert devices** | Hardware | Expensive ($30+/month); elderly-focused; stigma |

### Our Differentiation
1. **Simplicity**: One feature done perfectly
2. **Price**: One-time $1.99 (no subscription)
3. **Cross-platform**: Works on iOS and Android
4. **Automatic**: Set once, works forever
5. **Non-stigmatizing**: Modern app, not a medical device

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Low initial downloads | Medium | High | ASO, PR around solo living trend |
| High false positive alerts | Low | High | Grace period, escalating reminders |
| Twilio/SMS costs | Medium | Medium | Rate limiting, email fallback |
| Copycat apps | High | Low | First-mover advantage, brand |
| App Store rejection | Low | High | Follow guidelines carefully |

---

## Success Criteria for MVP

The MVP is successful if:

1. **Users complete onboarding** - >80% of installs add at least 1 emergency contact
2. **Users check in regularly** - >90% check-in completion rate
3. **Alerts work reliably** - 100% of missed check-ins trigger alerts
4. **Users recommend it** - NPS score >50
